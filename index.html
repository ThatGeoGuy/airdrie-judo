Welcome

Established in 1980, the Airdrie Judo Club is a fantastic local sports club for any age or ability level. Whether you want to introduce your child to his or her first sport, or to help an established athlete win a national championship, we have the resources to help you succeed.

With our team of several NCCP accredited coaches and dedicated volunteers, our club has managed to produce a multitude of provincial and national level athletes and competitors. Our complete facility includes an 80 tatami fighting area (approximately 130 square metres); cardio and weight training equipment; and a positive, encouraging atmosphere for our students and athletes to learn.

What is Judo?

Judo is a martial art and olympic sport invented in 1882, that has deep philosophical roots which extend to the core of the art. Even the name judo, which translates from Japanese to "the Gentle Way," embodies the idea that we can defeat any obstacle by taking the path of least resistance. Some core tenets of the philosophy of judo are Maximum Efficiency and Mutual Prosperity.

Nowadays, Judo is a competitive sport that is practiced world-wide, from children in the dojo to fierce athletes at international competitions, such as the Olympics. Anyone can learn from the techniques of Judo, regardless of age, size, or gender. Judo is an inexpensive, year-round activity that appeals to all walks of life; even for those not interested in competition, Judo is an excellent recreational sport that can stimulate the mind and improve your physical health while promoting positive moral values, self-esteem, self-confidence, and overall well-being.

Our Instructors

Our club is run by a team of dedicated volunteers, and of those our instructors, known as Sensei, are your first resource to learn about the club and Judo as a whole. Each of our instructors has more than 10 years experience in Judo, and many were once national and international competitors. Many of our instructors maintain certification through the National Coaching Certification Program of Canada.

Register

Our classes are split into three sections: pre-judo (ages 4-6), junior (ages 7-12), and senior (ages 12+). All of our classes are on Mondays and Wednesdays between 6:30PM and 9:30PM, except for pre-judo which is Wednesdays only. For more information on how we divide our classes, and what to expect, see our About Us page.

If you are interested in trying Judo, and want to register for one term (approximately 4 months), fill out one of our registration forms and bring it to our club. Even if you're unsure about joining, feel free to drop by and try a class, we'd be happy to help you learn what Judo is all about. If you do decide to come out, be sure to wear comfortable clothes and bring water! If you need directions, or want to contact us directly for more information, see our contact page for more details.
